Exp C: Benchmark Robustness and Container Overhead

Finding: the Native/MGC inconsistency is primarily a benchmark robustness issue, not evidence of a steady-state multi-GPU-container overhead. Cold ShareGPT samples and native Ray/vLLM startup variance can dominate TTFT and TPOT. After Exp A3 explicitly discards the first bench sample, Native and MGC are statistically tied, while PGC remains slower due to its degraded cross-container NCCL transport.

1. Question

Exp C was reopened because the ShareGPT saturation pass appeared to show a Native/MGC gap that contradicted the earlier conclusion that single-container overhead should be near zero. The refined question is: did the benchmark method create that apparent gap, or does MGC have a real steady-state serving penalty?

2. Root Cause of the Inconsistency

  1. First bench samples are cold-path measurements. They include request scheduler warmup, cache/prefix-cache initialization, EPLB movement, and post-start Ray/vLLM settling. They are not representative of steady serving.
  2. Native cold/warm spread is especially large. Native diagnostic samples saw TTFT fall from 1155–1461 ms on first benches to ~239 ms on a warm bench, with TPOT falling from ~111–113 ms to ~93 ms.
  3. ShareGPT is realistic but long-tailed. It exposes transport and scheduling behavior better than random 128/128, but each sample is long and noisy enough that warmup must be separated from the measured window.
  4. Previous summaries made fallback windows too easy to misread. If a run failed to converge, the report could treat a late window as stable while the trajectory still contained warmup drift. The harness now records stable_start_bench, discard_first, convergence status, and all per-bench trajectories.

5. Feedback to Exp A3

Exp A3 now treats benchmark robustness as part of the method, not a post-hoc interpretation:

  • A3_BENCH_DISCARD_FIRST=1 by default; discarded benches are excluded from convergence and summary statistics.
  • Default repeated-run parameters are max_benches=8, window_size=3, extra_samples=1, and eps_pct=5.
  • Reports use the measured warm window and show per-bench trajectories so cold-start contamination is visible.

6. Current Interpretation

There is no robust evidence of steady-state MGC container overhead. The apparent gap came from a benchmark method that mixed cold-start behavior with steady serving. The real transport-related result remains MGC versus PGC: after warmup filtering, PGC is 6.2% lower throughput and 11.4% higher TPOT than MGC on DP=4 ShareGPT c32, matching the NCCL evidence that PGC falls back to NET/Socket/0.
